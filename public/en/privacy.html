Information on the processing of your data

1. Data Protection at a Glance

General Information

The following information provides a simple overview of what happens to your personal data when you visit this website. Personal data is any data with which you can be personally identified.

Who is Responsible?

Data processing on this website is carried out by the website operator (OYSI GmbH). You can find the contact details in section 3 of this privacy policy.

How Do We Collect Your Data?

Your data is collected in part when you provide it to us (e.g. via email or contact form). Other data is collected automatically or with your consent when visiting the website by our IT systems (e.g. technical data such as browser, operating system, IP address).

What Do We Use Your Data For?

Some of the data is collected to ensure error-free provision of the website. Other data may be used to analyse your user behaviour – but only if you have consented to this.

2. Hosting

We host the content of our website with the following provider:

OVH

The provider is OVH GmbH, Dudweiler Landstraße 5, 66123 Saarbrücken, Germany (hereinafter "OVH"). OVH is a European cloud provider with data centres in the EU.

When you visit our website, your personal data is processed on OVH servers. This may include IP addresses, contact requests, meta and communication data, website access and other data generated via a website.

OVH holds ISO 27001 and ISO 27701 certifications, ensuring a high level of data protection. Data is processed exclusively in data centres within the European Union.

Legal Basis: The use of OVH is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR. We have a legitimate interest in presenting our website as reliably as possible. If consent has been requested, processing is carried out exclusively on the basis of Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR.

We have concluded a data processing agreement (DPA) with OVH. This is a contract required by data protection law, which ensures that OVH processes the personal data of our website visitors only according to our instructions and in compliance with the GDPR.

5. Analysis Tools (Google)

Google Consent Mode V2 (Basic Implementation)

We use Google Consent Mode V2 in the basic implementation. This service allows us to transmit your decision made in the cookie banner to Google.

Consent granted: Google Analytics collects and processes data in full.

Consent denied: No data is transmitted to Google and no cookies are set.

Consent Mode V2 controls the following parameters, which are set to "denied" by default:

  • ad_storage: Storage of advertising cookies
  • ad_user_data: Transmission of user data for advertising purposes
  • ad_personalization: Personalised advertising
  • analytics_storage: Storage of analytics cookies

Google Analytics 4

If you have given your consent, we use Google Analytics 4, a web analytics service provided by Google Ireland Limited, Gordon House, Barrow Street, Dublin 4, Ireland. Google Analytics uses technologies that enable user recognition for the purpose of analysing user behaviour (e.g. cookies or device fingerprinting).

The information collected by Google about your use of this website is usually transferred to a Google server in the USA and stored there. However, by activating IP anonymisation, your IP address is previously truncated by Google within member states of the European Union or in other contracting states of the Agreement on the European Economic Area.

Data Transfer to the USA: Google bases the data transfer to the USA on the EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF). The European Commission determined on 10 July 2023 that the USA provides an adequate level of data protection, provided that the company is certified. Google LLC is certified under the DPF.

More information: Google Privacy Policy

Legal Basis: Processing is carried out exclusively on the basis of your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR). You can revoke your consent at any time via the cookie settings.

6. Data Subject Rights

  • Objection (Art. 21 GDPR): You have the right to object to processing at any time for reasons relating to your particular situation.
  • Withdrawal of Consent: You can withdraw consent you have given at any time.
